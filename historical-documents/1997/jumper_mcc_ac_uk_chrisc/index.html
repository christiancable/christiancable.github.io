Born Halloween afternoon 1975 at St. James hospital Leeds in the UK, (21, over the hill I guess) moved to the sea side town of Blackpool in the early eighty's after short stop offs in Morley and St. Annes (a place with far too many old people to not suspect a sinister plot). On the way to now I've attended various educational establishments (namely Peel Street lower, Mayfield county primary, Roseacre, Highfield High and the Blackpool Sixth Form College) seeking wisdom and cunning ways to pass my time.

Currently I'm spending far too much time playing with computers (obviously a result of a misspent childhood in front of a rather hot ZX-81 and some wonderful teenage years playing with my Sam Coupe ) at The University of Central Lancashire in Preston (where it looks very likely I shall be returning to live!) where I'm the current chairman of CompSoc! "The Computer Society of The University of Central Lancashire" which amoung other things involves looking after Nexus. Nexus is a rather nifty BBS system on the university's PC network, written by past members of the society and is a lot more interesting than it first sounds. If you've got a java enabled web browser then you can check out the test version of the new web nexus!!! Hopefuly there should be some windows client software for non java folks out there soon. (depending on my uni work load, social life and the sufficient use of the force) We've also just started to provide web pages for our members, click here for the story so far

I also play bass in an almost all girl band known as Violet. We've been together for about a year and a half now and I'm told sound like a mixture of the pixies and fuzzbox. We've always on the look out for places to play ,although our wonderful singer Chantelle has just gone of to uni (come back I miss you!) so it'll have to be in the holidays I guess. One day I'll scan in some photos but not yet! Of course it goes without saying that if you want to know more about the band or want to obtain our demo tape (only £2:50 for 6 all new tracks) then please, please, please mail us! (especially if you know of any places looking for local bands to play, did you get the hint yet? :-) )
